About

Two disciplines, one problem

I'm a Mechatronic Engineer at a consulting engineering firm in Windhoek, working toward Pr.Eng registration. Day to day I design electronic and electrical building services: ICT networks, surveillance and access control, AV, UPS and generator backup, and LV distribution to SANS 10142-1. Mostly commercial offices and government buildings, with wastewater treatment, mining and rail work that extends into industrial automation: PLCs, instrumentation and SCADA. I handle contract administration where projects need it. The kind of work where a wrong diversity factor or a missed notice period has real consequences.

Before consulting: pharmaceutical automation (BMS, HVAC and environmental monitoring under GxP and FDA rules), agricultural IoT, remote support engineering for a US crypto company, and five years self-employed building bespoke IoT and gamification hardware, from the PCB up to the server stack.

I got tired of watching engineering work done by hand when most of it is pattern-matching against a standard. So I built a self-hosted multi-agent platform (nine agents, running on my own hardware: Directus/PostgreSQL, Chroma RAG over my notes, MCP tooling, Tailscale/Syncthing/Forgejo underneath) and started pointing it at the actual work: load calculations, contract clause lookups, document drafting. I author the Claude skills that encode that domain knowledge.

The interesting problems aren't in generic chatbots. They're in getting agentic AI to hold up inside workflows that are regulated, inconsistent, and unforgiving of hallucination. That's what I want to keep doing, for more than one employer's internal tooling.
